<Section icon={Swords} title="Combat Mechanics and Card Synergies">

Combat in Project Mirror Labyrinth relies on turn-based card play. You use Action Points (ATP) to play cards from your hand. To survive the increasingly difficult stages, you must maintain a healthy balance between offensive pressure and defensive mitigation.

### Understanding Card Types
Cards are divided into distinct categories, each serving a specific role in your tactical plan [00基础信息].

| Card Category | Core Function | Tactical Use Case |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Attack Cards** | Deal physical or elemental damage to targets | Deplete enemy HP and break their posture |
| **Defense Cards** | Generate block, shield, or barrier values | Mitigate heavy incoming damage during enemy turns |
| **Utility Cards** | Draw cards, generate ATP, or apply debuffs | Cycle your deck faster and weaken boss mechanics |

### Posture and ATP Management
Your character's combat efficiency relies on two primary resources: Posture and ATP (Action Points).
- **Posture**: Posture acts as a secondary defense bar. If your Posture is broken, you are stunned and take massive damage. Acquire the **Breathing** passive early to gain posture recovery per turn.
- **ATP**: You spend ATP to play cards. ATP is expanded by using **Condense** and upgrading your Outfits.
