<Section icon={Swords} title="Combat Fundamentals: Sanity, Clashes, and Posture">

The combat in Project Mirror Labyrinth revolves around card-based clashes. Unlike traditional RPGs where you simply trade damage, every attack in this game can be contested by the enemy. Winning these clashes is the single most important factor for early-game survival.

### The Power of Posture and Blocking
Your character relies on posture to execute actions. If your posture drops to zero, you become staggered and highly vulnerable. Managing this resource is critical when your deck options are limited.

<CardGrid cols={3}>
  <Card title="Winning Clashes">
    - **Sanity Generation**: Winning a clash is the primary way to gain sanity.
    - **Damage Prevention**: Prevents the enemy's card effects from triggering.
    - **Momentum**: Keeps your offensive pressure high.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Defensive Blocking">
    - **Zero Posture Cost**: Standard blocks cost zero posture to play.
    - **Posture Recovery**: Recovers 3 posture points upon use (5 with specific perks).
    - **Safe Turn**: Perfect for defensive turns when hand options are bad.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Poise & Crit">
    - **Damage Multiplier**: Poise can boost your total output up to 1.5x.
    - **Slash Synergy**: Works exceptionally well with slash-heavy Outfits.
    - **Caution**: Do not over-invest in Poise early; focus on raw clash power first.
  </Card>
</CardGrid>

To visualize how different combat actions affect your resources, refer to the comparison table below:

| Combat Action | Posture Cost | Sanity Impact | Best Used For |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Standard Attack** | Varies (Usually 1-3) | Increases on clash win | Dealing damage and winning clashes |
| **Block Card** | 0 | Neutral | Recovering posture and absorbing hits |
| **E.G.O. Skills** | High (12-15) | High cost / High reward | Winning high-rolling clashes |
| **Discard Actions** | Varies | Neutral | Cycling hand and triggering discard passives |

<Callout type="warning" title="Stagger Warning">
Never let your posture run completely dry. If you see an enemy preparing a high-rolling attack that you cannot beat, use a Block card to absorb the blow rather than contesting it with a weak attack.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Target} title="Step-by-Step Early Progression Guide">

To progress efficiently, you should follow a structured path. Do not linger in the Back Streets longer than necessary; the real power spikes lie deeper in the labyrinth.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Claim Your Starter Funds">
    Redeem all active codes (such as **Welcome** and **OfficeDelay**) in the settings menu to secure thousands of Fragments, Condense, and Seeds of Light.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Acquire a Solid Starter Outfit">
    Use your Fragments on Standard or Targeted Reflections to pull a reliable Outfit. Look for Outfits with high base rolls on their cards to make early clashes easier.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Clear the Back Streets">
    Enter the Back Streets dungeon via the TAB menu or the NO ENTRY gate. Focus on learning enemy patterns and farming posture using block cards.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Target HP Recovery Passives">
    Look out for specific enemies like the **Swaying Fixer** or **Cape Goon** in the Back Streets, as they drop passives that regenerate your HP.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Push Into the Library">
    Once you defeat the Back Streets boss, immediately head into the Library. This zone features enemies that drop the best permanent passives in the game.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="info" title="Progression Tip">
When choosing rewards before a boss fight, prioritize HP regeneration or Posture recovery if your resources are low. Only select card deletion/discard options if your deck is heavily bloated with basic cards.
</Callout>

</Section>

### Outfit Selection Strategy
When choosing which Outfit to run in the early game, focus on raw clash numbers rather than complex status effects like Burn or Bleed. High base power cards will carry you through early zones even if you get unlucky with coin flips.

| Outfit Tier | Outfit Name | Key Strength | Upgrade Priority |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **S-Tier** | KO Plus Two Security Chief | High rolls, excellent burn application, and strong block | High (Defragment immediately) |
| **A-Tier** | Modern War Office 7 | Consistent slash damage and solid clash power | Medium |
| **B-Tier** | Office Grade 7 | Decent starting deck but lacks high-end passives | Low (Replace when possible) |

<Callout type="success" title="Upgrade Recommendation">
Use your Seeds of Light and Condense to **Defragment** your S-Tier Outfits. This unlocks their latent passive abilities, which remain active throughout your entire dungeon run.
</Callout>
