<Section icon={Swords} title="Combat Mechanics and Deck Building Strategy">

Combat in Project Mirror Labyrinth is turn-based and revolves around playing cards from your hand to counter enemy actions. As you progress through a dungeon, you will add new cards to your deck, creating synergies that can either make or break your run. Understanding the distinction between card types is the first step toward mastering the meta.

<CardGrid cols={3}>
  <Card title="Offensive Build">
    - **High Damage**: Focuses on overwhelming enemies quickly.
    - **Fragile**: Requires precise timing to avoid lethal counter-attacks.
    - **Best for**: Fast farming and early dungeon floors.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Defensive Build">
    - **Sustainability**: Uses Block and Evade cards to mitigate damage.
    - **Slow Burn**: Battles last longer but are much safer.
    - **Best for**: Boss encounters and high-difficulty mazes.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Utility Hybrid">
    - **Adaptability**: Mixes resource generation with moderate damage.
    - **Complex**: Harder to pilot but handles diverse enemy types.
    - **Best for**: Experienced players seeking deep dungeon runs.
  </Card>
</CardGrid>

The card system is inspired by *Slay the Spire*, meaning you need to manage your "Energy" or "Cost" effectively. Playing too many high-cost attack cards might leave you defenseless during the enemy's turn.

| Card Category | Effect Type | Strategic Value |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Attack** | Direct Damage | **High**: Necessary to end fights quickly. |
| **Defense** | Shield / Block | **Critical**: Prevents HP loss during enemy turns. |
| **Buff** | Stat Increase | **Medium**: Enhances long-term combat efficiency. |
| **EGO** | Ultimate Ability | **Highest**: Can turn the tide of a losing battle. |

<Callout type="warning" title="Combat Warning">
Do not ignore defensive cards. In Project Mirror Labyrinth, health does not regenerate fully between every floor. A "glass cannon" build will often fail before reaching the final boss.
</Callout>

The core of the **project mirror labyrinth guide** involves navigating the maze-like dungeons. These are procedurally generated, meaning the path you take will change with every run. You must choose between different nodes, such as Combat, Elite Encounters, or Rest Sites.

  <Step num="3" title="Manage Encounters">
