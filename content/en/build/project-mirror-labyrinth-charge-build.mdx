<Section icon={Shield} title="Best Outfits for the Charge Build">

Your choice of outfit defines your starting deck, base stats, and passive capabilities. For a successful **project mirror labyrinth charge build**, you must aim for outfits belonging to the W-Corp or Cleanup Crew factions, as they directly facilitate Charge generation and expenditure.

### W-Corp L3 Cleanup Captain (Super Rare)
This Super Rare outfit is the absolute pinnacle for late-game Charge enthusiasts. Its signature card, **Rip Space**, consumes all accumulated Charge. When you play Rip Space with 10 or more Charge Count, it guarantees a successful clash and unleashes astronomical damage that can skip entire boss phases.

### Newbie Cleanup Agent (Rare)
Do not let the "Newbie" tag fool you; this outfit is highly competitive and incredibly fun to pilot. It comes equipped with two copies of **Overcharge**, a massive 15-roller card that requires zero build-up. It also features **Leap** for rapid deck cycling and **Energy Cycle** to punish staggered opponents.

| **Starter Outfit** | Common | Launch | Default option. Lacks Charge mechanics but offers stable basic clashing. |

<Section icon={Swords} title="Key Cards and Deck Composition">

A successful **project mirror labyrinth charge build** relies on a delicate balance between Charge generators and high-impact spenders. If you pack your deck with too many spenders, you will suffer from low clash rolls due to insufficient Charge. Conversely, carrying only generators will leave you lacking the offensive power needed to defeat elite dungeon bosses.

### Overcharge
Overcharge is a 3-intent card that rolls up to a 15. It hits three times (8 + 16 + 24) for a base total of 48 damage, making it a free mini-nuke against enemies preparing multi-intent skills. Because it requires no prior setup, it is highly reliable in early dungeon stages.

### Rip Space
The ultimate finisher for the W-Corp L3 Cleanup Captain. While it spends all Charge, reaching the 10-charge threshold turns this card into an unstoppable force that easily wins any clash in the game.

### Leap
A utility card that is vital for drawing your entire deck. Deck velocity is incredibly important in roguelite deck builders; Leap ensures you cycle back to your powerful Overcharge and defensive block cards quickly.

| Card Name | Posture Cost | Primary Effect | Strategy Tip |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Overcharge** | Low | Deals 8 + 16 + 24 damage across 3 intents. | Use to counter multi-intent enemy skills. |
| **Rip Space** | Medium | Spends all Charge for massive damage. | Only play when Charge Count is 10 or higher. |
| **Leap** | Low | High clash power and draws cards. | Play early in the turn to expand your hand choices. |
| **Energy Cycle** | Low | Deals bonus damage to staggered enemies. | Save for turns when the enemy's posture is broken. |

<Callout type="success" title="Deck Thinning Tip">
Always remove basic, low-value block cards from your deck at dungeon shops or rest sites. While blocking is useful for recovering posture, basic blocks dilute your deck and prevent you from drawing your high-value Charge generators.
</Callout>

</Section>
