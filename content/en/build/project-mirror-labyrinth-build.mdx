<Section icon={Swords} title="Combat Mechanics and Card Archetypes">

Combat in Project Mirror Labyrinth revolves around the strategic use of Attack and Defense cards. Every turn, you are presented with choices that determine whether you will mitigate incoming damage or push for a kill. A balanced **project mirror labyrinth build** typically maintains a 60/40 split between offensive and defensive capabilities.

| Card Category | Primary Function | Ideal Use Case | Strategic Value |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Attack Cards** | Deal direct damage | Finishing enemies quickly | **High** for clearing mobs |
| **Defense Cards** | Block/Mitigate damage | Surviving heavy boss hits | **Critical** for long runs |
| **Utility Cards** | Draw cards/Buff stats | Setting up massive combos | **Medium** for deck cycling |
| **EGO Cards** | Special high-impact moves | Turning the tide of battle | **Very High** limited usage |

<Callout type="warning" title="Combat Warning">
Ignoring Defense cards is the fastest way to end a run. Bosses in the deeper labyrinth floors have multi-hit attacks that can bypass poorly constructed decks. Ensure your build has at least three reliable "Block" or "Evasion" cards.
</Callout>

### Synergizing Cards with Outfits
Outfits are more than just cosmetic skins; they are the "class" system of the game. Each Outfit comes with innate stat modifiers that can amplify specific card types. For instance, an Outfit with high "Slash" resistance and "Pierce" damage bonuses should be paired with a deck that focuses on piercing attacks. Understanding these tags is essential for optimizing your character's output.

| Outfit Type | Best Card Synergy | Build Focus |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Striker** | High-damage physical cards | Burst damage/Fast clears |
| **Guardian** | Shield and Parry cards | Tanking/Attrition warfare |
| **Tactician** | Card draw and Energy regen | Combo-heavy/Endless turns |
| **EGO-Focused** | Special EGO ability cards | High resource/Late-game power |

### The Defragmenting Process
Once you have acquired a powerful Outfit, the next step is Defragmenting. This process requires rare materials like **Condense** and **Seeds of Light**. Defragmenting unlocks passive abilities that are permanent for that Outfit, providing a significant edge in harder dungeons.

| Material | Primary Source | Usage in Build |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Fragments** | Dungeon runs & Codes | Pulling new Reflections/Skills |
| **Condense** | High-level rewards | Initial Defragmenting tiers |
| **Seeds of Light** | Special events & Elite drops | Unlocking final Passive tiers |
| **Tickets** | Milestone rewards | Guaranteed Outfit pulls |

<Section icon={Shield} title="Advanced Strategies for Labyrinth Survival">

As you move into the mid-to-late game, your **project mirror labyrinth build** needs to account for complex enemy mechanics. Enemies will begin to use "Status Ailments" like Bleed or Burn, which can drain your health even if you block the initial hit.

### Status Effect Management
Understanding how to counter status effects is vital. Some Outfits provide natural resistance, while certain cards can "Cleanse" these debuffs.

| Status Effect | Impact on Player | Best Counter Strategy |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Bleed** | Damage when playing cards | Use high-value cards to play fewer total |
| **Burn** | Damage at the end of turn | Finish the fight quickly/Cleanse cards |
| **Paralysis** | Reduces card efficiency | Prioritize defensive cards until it clears |
| **Fragile** | Increases damage taken | Stack extra Defense cards or Shields |

<Callout type="tip" title="Pro Strategy">
In the later stages of a dungeon, prioritize "Card Draw" cards. The more cards you can see in a single turn, the more likely you are to find the specific defensive tool needed to survive a boss's ultimate attack.
</Callout>
