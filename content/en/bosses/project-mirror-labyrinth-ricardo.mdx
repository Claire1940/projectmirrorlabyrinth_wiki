<Section icon={Swords} title="Combat Mechanics and Ricardo Build Meta">

The combat in Project Mirror Labyrinth revolves around a turn-based card system where every choice matters. For a **project mirror labyrinth ricardo** style build, players should focus on cards that provide high "Stagger" damage and "Blunt" resistance. Unlike standard builds that might rely on bleed or burn effects, a Ricardo-inspired deck is about raw power and breaking the enemy's guard as quickly as possible.

<Callout type="warning" title="Clash Mechanics">
Always check the enemy's intended action before playing your cards. If an enemy is using a high-damage attack, you must prioritize **Defense cards** or high-roll **Attack cards** to win the "Clash" and avoid taking massive damage.
</Callout>

### Card Type Comparison

Understanding the balance between different card types is essential for maintaining your health throughout a long dungeon run.

| Card Category | Primary Function | Ricardo Synergy | Best Use Case |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Attack** | Deals direct HP damage | High (Focus on Blunt) | When enemy speed is low |
| **Defense** | Mitigates incoming damage | Medium | During high-damage boss turns |
| **Utility** | Draws cards or gains resources | Low | When hand size is depleted |
| **EGO** | Ultimate power-up | Very High | To finish off elite enemies |

By focusing on physical Outfits that enhance your base stats, you can turn a standard deck into a powerhouse capable of clearing the deepest layers of the labyrinth. Remember to use **Condense** to upgrade these cards during your run to keep up with the scaling difficulty of the enemies.

<Section icon={Target} title="Dungeon Progression: Step-by-Step Guide">

Entering the labyrinth is the core loop of the game. Whether you are farming for Fragments or testing a new **project mirror labyrinth ricardo** deck, following a structured approach to each run will maximize your efficiency.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Lobby Preparation">
    Start by checking the **Codes** menu on the left side of the screen. Enter all active codes to ensure you have enough Fragments for potential mid-run upgrades. Ensure your active Outfit is the one you wish to level up.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Entering the Maze">
    Press **TAB** to open the main menu and select **Play**, or physically walk your character to the **NO ENTRY** gate. This will teleport you to the dungeon starting area where your initial deck is generated.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Path Selection">
    Dungeons are non-linear. Choose paths that offer **EGO Gifts** or **Card Upgrades** early on. Avoid elite encounters until you have at least two or three strong synergies established in your deck.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Resource Management">
    During the run, you will earn temporary currency. Use this at rest sites to heal or to remove "dead" cards from your deck that don't fit your Ricardo build. A lean deck of 15-20 cards is usually better than a bloated one.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Boss Encounters">
    Bosses have unique mechanics. Observe their "Passive" buffs and adjust your card play accordingly. Winning a clash against a boss often triggers a "Stagger" state, allowing you to deal double damage for one turn.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="info" title="Maintenance Awareness">
If you are disconnected during a run, check the time. The game undergoes maintenance every **Tuesday at 2:00 P.M.** During this window, dungeon progress may not be saved, and servers will be inaccessible for a short duration.
</Callout>
